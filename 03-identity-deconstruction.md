# 03: IDENTITY DECONSTRUCTION

> "Who you think you are is a constructed layer."

## Core Principle
**Identity is learned, not original.**
Your "self" is a collection of inherited labels, social roles, and external expectations accumulated over time. This layer is not about building a "better" you — it is about identifying the **architecture of the false you**.

## Core Realization
> **You are not the identity. You are the observer of it.**

---

## Protocol
1. **Identify imposed roles and labels:** List every title you carry (professional, relational, social). Recognize them as functions — not essence.
2. **Remove inherited expectations:** Isolate desires and fears placed on you by family, culture, or system.
3. **Detach from social identity structures:** Observe how you modify behavior to maintain an image. Refuse the need to sustain the construct.

## Protocol Execution
* **Question:** Who is acting — the essence or the role?
* **Action:** Remove the label. Observe what remains.

---

## Outcome
* **Separation from constructed identity.**
* **Recognition that identity is a layer — not the source.**
* **Reduced vulnerability to social conditioning and manipulation.**
