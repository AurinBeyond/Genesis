# 05: INTERNAL AUTHORITY

> "Authority is not given. It is recognized."

## Core Principle

**All authority is internal.**
No system, structure, or external validation defines your decisions.

Authority is not something to gain —
it is something to stop outsourcing.

---

## Core Realization

You do not need permission.

You need recognition.

---

## Protocol

1. **Shift decision-making inward**
   Pause before seeking advice, approval, or confirmation.
   Identify what is already clear internally.

2. **Eliminate validation loops**
   Notice where you rely on others to confirm your choices.
   Interrupt the need for approval.

3. **Act from internal clarity**
   Make decisions without external reinforcement.
   Allow them to stand without justification.

---

## Protocol Execution

* **Question:** Am I deciding — or seeking permission?
* **Action:** Act without external confirmation. Hold the decision.

---

## Outcome

* **Stabilized internal authority**
* **Independence from external validation**
* **Clarity in decision-making**
